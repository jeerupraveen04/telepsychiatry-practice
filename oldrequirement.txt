Intake Form Build Instructions
The attached documents contain the full content of a psychiatric intake questionnaire. The sections have been split into multiple files only to make them easier to upload and process.
All sections must be combined into one continuous digital form in the final product.
Content Preservation
All patient-facing wording must remain exactly as written.
• Do not rewrite, summarize, simplify, shorten, or remove any patient-facing text.
• The form may be structured differently in the digital format, but the actual wording, sentences, answer choices, and symptom lists must remain unchanged.
• Any text labeled “Form Builder Instructions — DO NOT DISPLAY” should be used only for logic configuration and must not appear to patients.
Important:
DSM-style symptom checklists must remain exactly as written with each symptom listed as an individual selectable item. Do not combine, summarize, or restructure these symptom lists.
Required Question Behavior
All questions must be required, except for conditional questions that only appear when triggered by form logic.
If a question includes options such as:
• N/A
• None
• Prefer not to answer
those options should remain selectable, but the question itself should still be required.
Conditional Logic
The form includes multiple conditional logic triggers.
Examples include:
• Perinatal section displayed based on Sex Assigned at Birth
• Follow-up questions displayed when symptoms are endorsed
• Additional detail fields triggered by positive responses
These logic instructions are included throughout the document and must be implemented exactly as written.
Symptom Checklist Behavior
Some sections contain long symptom checklists (DSM-style screening lists).
These lists must:
• remain fully expanded when first shown
• not be collapsed, summarized, shortened, converted to dropdowns, or merged into single summary questions
• display each symptom as its own individually selectable item
Each symptom must remain separately selectable so that multiple symptoms can be endorsed.
If the platform allows it, completed symptom sections may collapse after completion only to make navigation easier, but all symptom items must remain individually selectable and available for review or editing.
Section / Page Structure
To reduce scrolling and improve completion rates, the form should be built using section breaks or separate pages rather than one extremely long scrolling page.
Recommended structure:
    1. Patient Information
    2. Insurance / Pharmacy / Provider Information
    3. Current and Past Psychiatric Medications
    4. Medical History
    5. Mental Health Symptom Screening
    6. Trauma History / Symptom Onset / Safety

The uploaded files were divided only for easier processing and must be reassembled into one complete intake form.
Progress Bar and Navigation
If the platform supports it, please enable a progress bar so patients can see how far along they are in the form.
Patients should be able to move forward and backward between sections to review and edit answers before submission.
Long forms should not appear as one overwhelming page if section-based navigation is available.
Google Forms / Long Form Stability
Because this is a long psychiatric intake form, please build it in a way that avoids common Google Forms issues.
To improve stability and usability:
• use section breaks/pages for major sections
• keep long DSM symptom lists within their own sections when possible
• avoid placing multiple long symptom checklists on a single page
• preserve all checkbox and radio button structures exactly as written
• do not replace symptom lists with dropdown menus or paragraph fields
If adjustments are required for platform compatibility, maintain the exact wording and clinical meaning.
Formatting and Readability
To improve legibility and navigation:
• section titles should be bold and clearly visible
• headings may use slightly larger font sizes than body text
• related questions should be grouped logically within each section
• spacing should allow the form to remain readable on both desktop and mobile
The overall design should remain clean, professional, and easy to read.
Branding
If possible, include:
• practice logo
• simple professional color styling consistent with the website
The form should remain professional and not overly decorative.
File Structure
The intake form content has been divided into multiple smaller sections for upload purposes only.
These sections must be combined into one continuous form in the final version.
There should not be artificial page breaks between uploaded files beyond the logical sections described above.
Output Requirements
The final form must be:
• shareable with patients digitally
• compatible with Google Forms or similar form distribution methods

Form Privacy Settings

• Respondents cannot see other responses

The form must ensure that each patient can only see their own responses while completing the form.


Initial Psychiatric Evaluation Questionnaire
Estimated completion time: 30–60 minutes
Important: This intake form, voicemail, fax, email, and secure messaging are not monitored 24/7 and should not be used for emergencies or urgent situations.
If you are in crisis, think you may harm yourself or someone else, are experiencing severe worsening of symptoms, or believe you are having a medical or psychiatric emergency, call or text 988, call 911, or go to the nearest emergency room immediately.
Submitting this form does not establish emergency services, after-hours coverage, or an immediate provider response. It also does not guarantee same-day review, urgent medication refills, or immediate treatment recommendations.
If you are running out of medication, experiencing withdrawal symptoms, or have another urgent concern, you should also contact your current prescriber, primary care provider, urgent care, emergency department, or 988/911 as appropriate.
Please complete this form as accurately as possible. Your responses help your provider understand your history and prepare safely for your appointment.

How to Complete This Form
You may complete this form using your mouse or keyboard.
Using your mouse
    • Click circles or checkboxes to select answers
    • Click in text boxes to type responses
Using your keyboard
    • Press Tab to move to the next question
    • Press Shift + Tab to move to the previous question
    • Use ↑ ↓ arrow keys to move between answer options
    • Press Spacebar to select a checkbox or option

Important: Medical Records, Labs, and Monitoring
If you have recent medical records, laboratory results, imaging, urine drug screens, or medication history from another provider, please send them before your appointment if possible.
Providing these records ahead of time may help your provider:
    • better understand your overall health
    • identify medical contributors to mental health symptoms
    • avoid unnecessary repeat testing
    • make medication decisions more safely
    • avoid delays in treatment changes
You may send records using one of the following options:

Please do not use Spruce secure messaging, fax, email, voicemail, or this intake form for urgent or emergent concerns. These communication methods are intended for routine administrative information and records only and are not monitored continuously.
Some medications require periodic laboratory monitoring, including:
    • Lithium
    • Valproate / Depakote
    • Carbamazepine
Recent labs may be required before medication adjustments can be made. Providing recent labs in advance, if available, can help avoid delays in treatment changes.
Laboratory results may also be helpful because some medical conditions and vitamin deficiencies may contribute to mental health symptoms, including:
    • thyroid disorders
    • anemia
    • vitamin deficiencies
    • hormonal changes
    • metabolic conditions such as diabetes or high cholesterol
If you are currently receiving treatment for opioid use disorder (OUD) or are prescribed medications such as buprenorphine, methadone, or naltrexone, your provider may require a recent urine drug screen (UDS) before initiating or continuing certain controlled medications.
If you have had a recent UDS through another provider, please ask that provider to forward the results prior to your appointment if possible.
Providing these results ahead of time may help avoid delays in treatment planning.
